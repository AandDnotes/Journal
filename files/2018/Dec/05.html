Wednesday 5th December

This morning, while Angela was down in Steyning, I replaced the battery in the Macbook Air. All went well, except that I 'lost' one of the tiny screws that holds the bottom of the device in place. I had carefully placed each screw on the folded dust sheet, next to the hole, but I must have knocked one off the sheet. In spite of a good search I couldn't find it. So for the moment the Macbook Air is short of one screw. However, the system now says that the battery is in a 'normal' condition. We just need to see if it lasts longer.

As usual on a Wednesday Angela went to table tennis after lunch. It was their 'competition', but she didn't win! Meanwhile I wrote my Christmas cards and emailed Julie and Dave about the January newsletter, as well as doing more clearing out.

After an early (and quick) dinner we walked down to Steyning for the Steyning Christmas 'late night'. Luckily the rain stopped and there were a lot of people in the High Street and lots of stalls. There was even a fun fair in the car park with quite large rides. We called in to Osbourne Humphries for a glass of wine and then went into Truffles for a coffee. We were home before nine o'clock.
