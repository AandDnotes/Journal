Thursday 26th March

A sunny, breezy and chilly day (unless you are out of the wind in the sunshine). I contacted two local plumbers, one in Hove and one in Beeding. The one from Hove came before lunch, suggested that the best option was to run a new copper pipe around the house, going through walls in three places. Just after lunch he sent a thorough quotation! When here he said that he might be able to do the work tomorrow, if he could leave his mate to finish the current job.

I spent time preparing the photo for the RCEA newsletter report on Mike W's talk on his houseboat. Then I merged them with his text in the April newsletter.

Around five o'clock the Beeding man arrived. He suggested that we first investigate if the area around the existing pipe could be excavated to expose the elbow joint, which may be the source of the leak. That joint could be remade and might solve the problem. If it doesn't, then it would be necessary to run a new pipe around the building. I decided try this approach first. It could save £570, time and appearance. The Beeding plumber will come to try this tomorrow afternoon.

I had planned to take the B to Cowfold for Lackfords to look at the cills. However, the man I have to see had to go out. So I will try going tomorrow morning if the weather is not too wet.
