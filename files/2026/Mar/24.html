Tuesday 24th March

The day started dry, albeit cloudy and chilly, but then became wet after lunch. Angela went to the High Street while I worked through more of the savings accounts to find notices of interest.

A little later I took the B to get some petrol. I was concerned that the fuel guage always seemed to be indicating half full. I thought I would fill the tank and then fill it to that level each time. That should give a better indication of mpg. The pump nozle went fully in and I filled until the auto-stop happened. Then I continued filling (very briefly) until the second auto-stop. The guage then showed three-quarters full. In fact, the 13 litres that I put in is very close to a quarter of the tank's 54 litres capacity. So all seems to be well. I just need to see that the guage goes below the half filled mark.

After lunch I spoke with Graham Stevens, who restors MGs at his garage the other side of Lewis. He seems to know what he's talking about, but is now away until after Easter, two weeks away. So I said that I would phone him then.

Then it was back to interest statements. It's a pity that, after including the three accounts that I'd left out, I 'earned' quite a bit more interest than I'd thought. So I'm only a few thousand less than the figure used by HMRC. As I went through the accounts I printed off the statement. So I have all the details requested by the HMRC and I now just need to produce a covering letter.
