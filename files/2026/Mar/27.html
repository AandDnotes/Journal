Friday 27th March

Another busy, but cold (inside without gas) day. While Angela went to the High Street I did some of the IET 'data protection' training course.

Then I took the B to Lackfords at Cowfold, a 29-mile round trip, for them to look at the rear of the sills. They said that the rear outer part of each sill needs replacing, as well as the filler panel. They would also spray Waxoyl into the sills and box sections and touch-up the 'stonechip' underseal. They will send me a quotation (by post), but could not do any work until July.

After lunch Mr Willett arrived. He removed a little of the floor around the gas pipe and found a hole in it. The hole was about 3mm in diameter after he put his screwdriver through it. Later he sent an invoice for his investigation and two 'estimates'; one to excavate more of the floor, cut off the corroded piece of pipe, fit a compression joint and connect it to the meter and the other to run copper pipe right round the house. The former option is less than half the cost, but may not solve the problem. The latter is expensive and is not very visually attractive. However, he could do the latter option on Wednesday.

So we spent another day with only two fan heaters and the bathroom radiator heater.
