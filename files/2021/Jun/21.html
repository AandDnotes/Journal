Monday 21st June

The summer solstice. Not that you would know it. Apparently it was the coldest summer solstice for forty years, And it rained for a lot of the day. We just hope that the weather is a little better tomorrow, when we meet Kate and Dick for a picnic.

Angela went off for a coffee morning at the church, while I phoned Dad and then phoned NS&I about their demand for a repayment of an alleged over-payment. It seems that the letter was legitimate and they will send more details. I did more sums and found that for the second year of the three-year bond they had used a slightly incorrect interest rate, but that only made an over-payment of £13, rather than the £600+ figure they stated.

I then had a call from Julie, the manager at Victoria Grand. It seems that Dad has a urinary infection, but it seems that he has been going into a ladies bedroom (on the top floor) and has been found rubbing her back. They have asked him not to go into other rooms and wanted me to talk to him.

Finally I got around to some painting, putting gloss on the windowsill and a second coat on the new skirting board and trim. After lunch I painted the door. I can't get a good finish with the water-based gloss. The cupboard door look terrible.

I phoned Victoria Grand, but Dad was feeling unwell, so they suggested I phoned back after dinner, which I did. He was feeling a lot better and is on a course of antibiotics. I mentioned 'Mavis' and he said she had invited him in, but he has been told that he shouldn't go into her room. I then spoke to Alan to give him an update.
