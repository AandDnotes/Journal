1 December 2014
Monday

We decided last night that Victoria's cold was too heavy to attempt to go on the train to Sacramento. So we got up late and drove to Woodland for lunch. In so doing we drove past the Syngenta site. We also saw where they had lived before moving to Davis.
I booked a B&B in Placerville for the next two nights, even though it is going to be very wet.

2 December 2014
Tuesday

This morning, after Victoria had taken Austin to nursery school, she took us downtown to pick up our hire car. A red Ford Fiesta. We then set off along Interstate 80 towards Sacramento. Then on to US50 for a short while before turning off on to California 16 and joining 49, which would take us to Placeville. We stopped for a coffee at Plymouth before going on to Placeville, where we arrived at around one o'clock. The Sacramento valley is very flat, but the drive from Plymouth to Placeville was more interesting, since there were real hills and winding roads. We had lunch in Placeville and then walked the length of Main Street, in the rain! Out feet got quite wet. The B&B seemed quite adamant that check in was not until four o'clock, so we sat in the car until then! After a glass of wine and a look at the menus of various restaurants, we walked across the footbridge to Main Street and went to a Mexican restaurant.

3 December 2014
Wednesday

We had a leisurely start, with breakfast at nine o'clock (the Shafsky version of eggs benedict - English muffin, scrambled egg, sautéed vegetables, sprinkle of chilli powder, two spears of asparagus, cherry tomatoes marinaded in lemon juice and olive oil). We then walked down to the town. We went into the hardware store; quite an amazing shop; and bought a small wooden rolling pin and a Christmas-tree decoration. We then went on to look around the small museum. After that, we returned to the B&B and drove to an apple farm and to a winery. The first apple farm was very disappointing, being little more than a large shed with some apple-based products for sale. The second was a little better in that they had some picnic tables under cover. So we stopped for a coffee and a cake. We then went on to the Madrona winery, where we tasted several red and white wines and bought three bottles to take back to Victoria's. From there, we returned to Placerville and went up 49 to Coloma, where the Californian gold rush started. Quite a nice bendy road. But it started raining heavily when we got there, so we drove around a scenic route in the hills. Steph at the B&B said we were 'brave' to drive that route. By the time that we got back to Coloma the rain had eased and so we stopped for a coffee before heading back to Placerville. Later, after a glass of wine, we walked down to the town for a meal (in the Buttercup Pantry where they served 'lighter', but uninspiring dishes.

4 December 2014
Thursday

We had an exciting day today. It started as planned. We had breakfast, packed and set off on back roads towards a place called Folsom (I forgot to take a photo on my phone, so couldn't upload any). Folsom has a huge reservoir and an old town. The journey took less time than we thought and so we were back in Davis for lunch, arriving back at Victoria's at about 2:30. We decided to take the hire car back and then pick up Austin. Then the fun started.
When we got to Downtown Davis, the police had closed it off! It seems that there was a demonstration taking place. So we could not get to the car hire place! We got a little lost on the University campus and then phoned the hire company. But their suggested route was also blocked off. Eventually we persuaded the police to let us into a garage forecourt and out the other side into the road we wanted. We then had to walk to the railway station (about 20 minutes) to meet Victoria (and Austin). The whole episode took about two hours.
However, we had a good meal when we got back and now Austin is in the bath.ï»¿

Friday

This morning we drove into Davis and caught the Amtrack train into Sacramento. It only takes 15 minutes, but we went on the upper deck and Austin was really engrossed. We looked at Sacramento Old Town and the State Capitol building. We were back in Davis by four o'clock.

Saturday

Victoria received an e-mail from Brandon (in Thailand) saying that Woodland were holding their 'Holiday Parade' today. So after breakfast we drove to Woodland and spent two and a half hours watching the parade. Entertaining, but a little long. We went home for a late lunch and then went to Trader Joe's to buy some food and a cool bag.
In the evening I was able to watch the two episodes of Shield that I will miss due to being away.

We also checked-in to our flights for tomorrow.
