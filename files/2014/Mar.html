Saturday

Our usual walk to the newsagents at Beeding was followed by reading the paper!
I then started coding my revised design for the RC ground station.
After lunch we went to Bay's to assemble her new bird table. This took more effort than I thought it would. Angela also spent more time unblocking Bay's paper shredder than she expected. 2 March 2014
Sunday

After another walk to Beeding (and reading of the paper), I set out to do some practice flying. However, there was a new version of the simulation software, which took a long time to install. It does seem better, even though the planes respond differently. They now seem to be more like the real thing!
I then did the weekly and monthly accounts.
After lunch I completed the coding of the revised ground station software, but have yet to try compiling it.

3 March 2014
Monday

This morning I compiled my revised ground station code and got it working with the state model based plane software. But the string of receive timeouts remains. However, I found that the number of timeouts is equal to the maximum errors on the plane unit and it seems that a receive timeout on the plane is causing repeated timeouts until the ground station re-initialises.
After an early lunch I went to Dad's to help him change his bed. I then went on to George Turnbull's to meet with him and Hamish to discuss the planned IET industry meeting in Crawley.
This evening I started redesigning the plane software in the same style as the revised ground station.

4 March 2014
Tuesday

Where did today go? I started by downloading '12 Years a Slave' in the form of a Kindle book, from Amazon, using a Daily Mail voucher. As part of this I had to download the Kindle app.
I had worked out a possible scenario that would result in the problem that I was seeing with my RC software. And I found the problem. I was aborting receive mode when I should not have been! So I had the new design on the ground and the old design on the plane, but still with quite a lot of single receive timeouts on the ground. So I completed the design and coding for the plane with the new design that does not use interrupts. However, I am now getting some transmit errors on the plane that result in a receive timeout and a cycle time of up to 37ms. There also seems to be an occasional glitch of the servos.
Angela went to her table tennis club lunch today and had a good time.
After lunch I started reading the papers for Friday's DAG meeting.

Wednesday

I spent a good part of the morning looking at the DAG accounts to see if there are any important trends. But consistent and complete data is scant. However, I have a graph of annual values and later finished reading the documents.

7 March 2014
Friday

A relatively busy day. It started with a DAG meeting at Brighton. Even though it didn't start until ten, it meant leaving at 9:15. I was back just after 12:30, so lunch was at about the usual time.

Going to the far side of the front bedroom caused the signal to be lost! I put the ground unit on the study window sill and took the plane down the garden. It reached the bottom (about 45m), but dropped when I went behind the tree. With line of sight it is supposed to manage a kilometre.
After dinner I went to the flying club meeting at Lancing Leisure Centre. There were a lot of people there, including Les andDerek, and some good displays. I made a number of notes.

In the process I discovered that the short in my original folded dipole was at the plug. Unfortunately I had already cut off the loop and balun by this time!
I also tried flying a quad copter and a helicopter on the simulator. The quad copter was easy, but the heli was impossible!
