Monday 3rd February

It was sunny this morning while we visited Dad at Victoria Grand. We had taken the iPad to show him a couple of new photos, but he was quite taken by my family history pages. And when I showed him a page about a trip to Dorset he remembered the name of Durdle Door from seeing the photo. It clouded over during the morning and was raining by mid-afternoon.

We went on to Waitrose, having coffee and buying more than we had intended (although things we needed) and it was after midday by the time we got home.

After the fist shower Angela went out and washed the car! It needed it, but it was still spitting with rain while she did it. I enquired about the house in Menai Bridge and, without realising it, reserved it for 24 hours for the dates I had enquired. Paul said those dates were fine and Victoria later said they would work for them. I also sent off an outline of the Energy Watch workshop to see if it is what Slindon College have in mind.

Then I removed the doors of the 'boiler' cupboard and emptied it. Later I started trying to dismantle the shelving inside it.
