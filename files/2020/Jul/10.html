Friday 10th July

After a better night's sleep my groin was feeling easier. I could lift my right leg high enough to go up a stair step. Nevertheless, I didn't go out walking. Angela went to the butchers to buy enough meat to prepare and freeze for the time when we don't have an oven. Meanwhile I finished my email to the IET Sussex committee and replied to Greg about his first draft of next year's budget.

Angela cooked for the rest of the morning and after lunch I went into the garage. I stripped and soldered the ends of the horn wires, fitted the horn and connected it up. When I took the horn off I noticed that the word 'Alette' on the rim was up-side-down. I made the same mistake. I had fitted the rim assuming that the wire terminals would be at the top, but they need to be at the bottom. So I now have 'Alette' up-side-down! But it works. The plastic horn (also a Lucas) is still under the bonnet. When I went to remove the wire to the control box it pulled away, so wasn't very secure.

In the evening I added a final page on the horn to my notebook.
