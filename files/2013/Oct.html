Calke Abbey (built on the site of a priory) is a country manor house that was owned by an eccentric series of baronesses who collected various things, including many stuffed animals, but simply shut up the rooms when they could no longer maintain them. The NT have repaired the house, rather than restore it. Although they have restored the wallpaper in one of the rooms. There was also extensive grounds with a deer park and a large walled garden.
When we arrived we sat outside in the sunshine and had a bottle of wine. We also had our lunch outside and finished with a cup of tea in the car park.
