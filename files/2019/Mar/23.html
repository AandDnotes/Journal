Saturday 23rd March

After a reasonable night's sleep we pottered in the garden while Paul and Krista dealt with the ponies. We then all went to Delft, about a 40 minute drive. Paul had pre-booked a parking ticket and we were able to park just a short walk from the centre. Delft is a typical Dutch town, with old, tall buildings criss-crossed with canals, a large central square and multiple churches. We had lunch in a converted warehouse and then, shortly after, stopped for coffee and cake in a backstreet cafe. The weather was grey and not very warm, so we were back in Berkenwoude by four.

We then spoke with Victoria et al. They are on Summer time and we are an hour ahead of the UK, so we caught them at breakfast-time as usual. We then had some tea and played a 'drawing' game until it was Rowan's bedtime. It was my turn to read some of Harry Potter!
