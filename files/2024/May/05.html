Sunday 5th May

The day started sunny. I took the TC down to the High Street for the Vintage Revival. I had been offered a spot in the Star car park, so I went there at 9:15 via the bypass. It was quiet there.

After walking to the other end of the High Street I met the organiser and arranged to use one of the spare spots left by people pulling out. So I moved the TC to opposite the old Barclays Bank. It was very busy and I spent all morning talking to other car owners and to visitors. Then Angela came by and, after walking up and down the High Street, we walked home for lunch.

There was some drizzle, so I put the hood up before we went home. After lunch I walked back down. It was still busy, in spite of the occasional drizzle. I displayed a small sign saying that the car was for sale and one of the other car owners (he was showing a TR6) expressed some interest. Earlier I had given the owner of the car next to me some water, but when we came to leave, his car would not start. So I moved the TC and 'rounded-up' four other men to help push the car into parking lane. He arranged for a recovery service to rescue him.

When I got home we spoke to Paul and then to Victoria. Then I dried off the TC.

Overall it was a good day.
