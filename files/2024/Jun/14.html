Friday 14th June

It was mainly dry today, with only one short spell of rain. And it was an interesting day.

After emailing someone who wants to join my PEI group, I went into the garage. I started by cleaning the spark plugs and then removed the contact points, cleaned them and refitted/adjusted them. Then I took the car for a short run to warm the engine and then adjusted the timing. I did this by listening to the engine, retarding it a little from the current setting. A second test run indicated that the engine was running well. I had a slight problem with the speedo gearbox on the dynamo coming loose and fouling the distributor.

In the middle of this I joined a Teams meeting with a couple of students who are interested in setting up an IET group at the University of Brighton.

Earlier I had downloaded what was described as 'a horrible scan of the production records transcribed by Dick Knudson while he was at the factory in the 60s.' from the mg-tabc forum. When I looked for the record for TC4985 I found that it was listed with the current engine number, not what I had thought was the original engine. The T Register database shows a different engine number. I put a message on the forum to ask if the engine was changed before it left the factory.

Later in the evening I phone Venkatesh, who has moved to the UK and is living in Milton Keynes. He is with the same company (Cognisant), but seems to be responsible for Europe and Australia!
