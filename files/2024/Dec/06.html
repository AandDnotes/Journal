Friday 6th December

For the most part it was a sunny, calm and clear day, if a bit cold. I started by putting my Christmas letters into cards and writing the envelopes. Later in the day Angela took them and others down to the Post Office to post.

Later in the morning I went to have a haircut. It's a pity that she cut one side shorter than the other, but I will try to trim it myself. Whilst out I bought 50 2nd-class stamps for our Christmas cards. When I got home I found an email from Deshi with a response from the Estates people at the UoS. They have a greed to cancel our parking fines. So I appealed to my fine and passed on the details to the list of attendees. Before I had finished that email I received one that said my appeal had been successful.

Whilst clearing out some of the accumulated email I found one from Mike W pointing out a typo in the newsletter. So I had to quickly correct it and send out the pdf file again.

Finally I started writing the notes from yesterday evening's PEI group meeting. Although I only got as far as setting up the framework.

All-in-all it has been a tiring day. And the next named storm (Darragh) is due this evening and overnight.
