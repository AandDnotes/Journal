Friday 3rd June

Both an interesting and a sad day. We spent the day at the SADCASE Classic Car event at Amberley Museum and Dad passed away.

We arrived at Amberley in the TC earlier than expected, about 9:30, but there were already a lot of cars. We parked up with the other WSMGOC cars. During our walk around to view the cars, just before 11:30, I had a call from Caroline at Victoria Grand. Dad had complained of chest pains, so they called the paramedics who said he had erratic heart beats and a low blood pressure. They recommended going to hospital, be he didn't want to go. So the carers kept an eye on him and later found him slumped in his chair.

Since he had not seen a doctor within the last two weeks, his death is classed as unexplained. So Victoria Grand had to inform the police. They asked a local undertakers to look after his body. I spoke to them later and we now just need to wait for the coroner's office to contact us. The only other thing I did, apart from contacting relatives, was to cancel his newspapers.

I phoned Alan from Amberley, but the signal was not good. When we got home (about 3:30) I phoned Alan again, then emailed Victoria and Paul, phoned Aunt Joan and Beryl and emailed Anthony, Martin and John.
